E ho ro mo run am fearran
E ho ro mo run am fearran
Mo ghaol a' Mhorairne bh\`oidheach
Far an robh mi \`og nam leanabh

'S b\`oidheach tha d\`a thaobh Loch Suaineart
Sr\`on an t-Sithein grianach aine,
Laudail an Gleann 's Rathuaidhe
Far an robh an "Doctair Ruadh" na leanabh

'S 'o bh\`arr an t-Sithein chithinn c\`omhla
Gach gleann, loch is eilanan b\`oidheach
Tir a' bhainne 'n fhe\`or 's a' bhairich

Nam b\`iodh ceartas aig lichd cosnaidh
'S gun na h-uachdarain bhith cho moiteil,
'S mi nach fhagadh tir an t-Soisgeil
Airson fortan r\`ioghachd aineoil.

Sorraidh bhuam thar chuan gu m' ch`\airdean
gu m' luchd-d\`uthch' 's gach comunn G\`idhlig
'N latha ch\`i 's nach fhaic 's go br\`ath
Gu math sl\`an na m\`iltean beannachd

E ho ro my love is the land
E ho ro my love is the land
My love is Beautiful Morven
Where I was a young child

Beautiful are the two sides of Loch Sunart
Sunny, green, Strontian
Laudale, the Glen and the Rahoy
Where the "Red-Haired Doctor" was a babe

From the Sithean I would see together,
every glen, loch, and lovely island
Beinn Shianta yellow-colored with daisies,
land of milk, of grass, of sprouting branches

Were there justice for workers,
and were not he landlords so haughty,
I should never have left he land of the gospel
to seek my fortune in a strange country

Farewell over the sea to my close ones,
to my own people and every gaelic group
whether we meet or not and forever,
good health and a thousand blessings
