\Árde Chuain

Appears On : Dail-Riata
Language : Gàidhlig (Scottish Gaelic)
Lyrics: English Translation:
NÁAirde Chuain I wish I was in Airde-Chuain
Rèeibh ud tha fad' uam, Beside yon mountain that is so far from me
B'annsa leam a dhol air chuairt, I would love to go on a visit
gò`omhnaich to the cuckoo glen (glendun) on a Sunday
Aò`o And oh, oh all of Ireland
\òo Ireland, sadness and o,
'òonach my heart is heavy and sad
Iì`in Many's a christmas I myself was there
'ì`ill in Cushendun without a care
Aàain playing hurling on the white beach
Mòorn leam my white hurley stick in my hand
Nì`in Aren't I weary here, all alone and by myself?
Nò`on-dubh no draon, Here I don't hear cry of cock, blackbird, or corncrake,
gò`orach, an naosg fh`\ein, sparrow, thrush, or even the snipe
iò`omhnach and I can't tell which day is Sunday
Nach e an caothach seo ' tha buan This is an endless struggle,
Ièeigeas cluan and it makes life an illusion
Dhealaicheas ' chaora on uan It seperates the sheep from the lambs
'òoige and it seperates me from my youth
Nàaimh If I only had a boat and an oar,
Làaimh, to row over the waves,
Aà`an hoping to God to arrive safely
IÈ`Eirinn and so to die in Ireland
